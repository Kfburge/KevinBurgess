Coursework


Language Coursework

Japanese 1010-2020

These courses acted as the foundation of my Japanese langauge learning including vocab, grammar, speaking, writing, and listening-skills. Classes would gradually increase in language use as the semesters progressed. Projects, presentations and speaking practice were part of the curriculum to help me develop my skills along with frequent quizzes and tests.

Japanese Conversation and Composition 3050-3060

This course focused on developing Japanese language skills by applying them to conversation practice and writing compositions including recipes, discussing opinions on topics, and making arguments.

Japanese for International Business 3160-4160

These courses aimed at teaching common Japanese business communication as well as the cultural business customs of a Japanese business. Attention is paid to differences in advertising and workflow from American businesses through case studies of Japanese business and branch divisions such as Toyota, Coca cola, and Nintendo.

Language in Japanese Society 3980

Course that focused on Japanese linguistics including cultural perceptions towards language, regional dialects, language discrimination, and phonetical variations of speech. In this course I wrote papers on topics such as loanwords and language identity.

Introduction to Japanese Literature (In progress) 4060

This course focused on examining famous Japanese works of literature and media in the original language. Class is taught fully in Japanese, and students discuss a work’s themes, language, and ideas in Japanese.

Culture Coursework

History of Modern Japan 3330

Expansive course covering Japanese history from the establishment of the Tokugawa Shogunate in 1600 to today (course was taken in 2019). Covers historical developments of Japanese government and economy as well as social trends and their effects on the live of Japanese citizens. Dives into the birth and influence of the Zaibatsu as well as key figures after the end of WWII.

Economic Geography 3020

Course which focused on analyzing the economic impact of international policy and history on a region’s opportunity and struggles. Students take a critical view on the policy of international actors such as the IMF and the World Bank on countries economic success and failings

Japanese Culture 4990

Course that discussed various cultural aspects of Japanese society, including work culture, consumer tastes, legal differences, and pop culture.

Business Coursework

Financial Accounting Concepts 2010

An introductory course to accounting that focuses on teaching important terminology and how to use a balance sheet and the various steps one can use to ensure correct and ethical reporting.

Macroeconomics 2120-3150

A course which focuses on applying previously learned concepts to real world and hypothetical situations. Delves into monetary policy to understand the importance of currency valuation and its place in the global market structure as well as international actors such as the IMF and the World Bank.

Microeconomics 2110-3140

A course which focuses on applying previously learned concepts to real world and hypothetical situations. Dives further into market influences as well as the game theory of economic interaction. Marginal analysis and value proposition are elaborated and developed.

Managerial Economics (In progress) 3060

A course where I learned about applying economic concepts and tools including econometrics to forecast business growth and find inputs that attain profit maximization.

Consumer Behavior 3020

A course that takes a closer look into the consumer side of marketing by examining consumer motivation. Case studies are used to illustrate the various incentives and disincentives of marketing strategies as well as ethical and unethical strategies.

Promotional Strategy 4230

A course that investigates the various forms of promotional marketing as well as their advantages and disadvantages. Cases studies are used throughout to highlight these strategies and students are expected to replicate these strategies to hypothetical situations and markets.

International Marketing 4270

A marketing course focused on examining marketing in foreign target markets. Case studies are use to highlight potential oversights and mistakes of failure marketing strategies as well as successful brands that were able to carve out large market shares by using cultural competency.
